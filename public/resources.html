Resources: Official Links & Tools by Function

Find the right government resource or MediPrimer guide for whatever you're trying to do.

Below is a curated directory of official government resources and MediPrimer's own guides, organized by function. Every external link goes to a federal or state .gov website, SHIP (the official State Health Insurance Assistance Program), or NAIC (the National Association of Insurance Commissioners). All phone numbers are official. If you don't see what you're looking for, Getting Help can point you to local assistance.

All external links go to official .gov, SHIP, or NAIC sites. MediPrimer is not affiliated with Medicare, Medicaid, CMS, any state agency, or any insurance company. When in doubt, verify details with the official source or your plan documents.

Get Free, Unbiased Help

Medicare Personal Help Line

1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) | TTY 1-877-486-2048 | medicare.gov

Free, official, unbiased support from Medicare specialists. Questions about enrollment, coverage, costs, appeals, or switching plans. Open year-round.

State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP)

1-877-839-2675 | shiphelp.org

Free counseling for Medicare beneficiaries, available in every state. Local advisors help you understand your coverage, compare plans, and file complaints. No sales pressure.

Eldercare Locator

1-800-677-1116 | eldercare.acl.gov

Connects older adults and caregivers to local resources: aging agencies, home-care providers, legal aid, and community programs. Run by the Administration for Community Living.

MediPrimer: Getting Help

mediprimer.org/getting-help.html

An overview of free assistance programs, how to choose a counselor, and what to expect. Includes tips for navigating complex enrollment or appeals situations.

MediPrimer: SHIP Directory

mediprimer.org/ship-directory.html

Find your state's SHIP office and local counselors. Includes phone numbers, hours, and what to bring when you reach out.

Apply for or Enroll in Coverage

Medicare Enrollment

medicare.gov

Check eligibility, apply online, and manage your enrollment. Includes Original Medicare (Parts A & B), Part D (prescription drug), and Medigap enrollment.

Social Security (SSDI, Retirement, and Medicare)

1-800-772-1213 | ssa.gov

Apply for Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI), retirement benefits, and get help with automatic Medicare enrollment. Workers with disabilities at any age and retirees at 62+ start here.

Healthcare.gov (ACA Marketplace)

healthcare.gov

Apply for ACA Marketplace coverage, check subsidies, and find plans from private insurers. Open Enrollment (typically Nov 1–Jan 15) and special enrollment periods for qualifying events.

State Medicaid Programs

mediprimer.org/state-medicaid.html

Apply for Medicaid or CHIP in your state. Eligibility and enrollment vary significantly by state; this directory has links to each state's portal and phone number.

Insure Kids Now (CHIP)

insurekidsnow.gov

Find and apply for CHIP coverage for children and teenagers. Includes links to state programs and a live chat helpline.

MediPrimer: Enrollment & Deadlines

mediprimer.org/enrollment.html

Understand Open Enrollment, Special Enrollment Periods, Initial Enrollment Period, and why deadlines matter. Includes consequences of missing deadlines.

MediPrimer: Enrollment Calendar

mediprimer.org/enrollment-calendar.html

Key dates for Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, and ACA Marketplace enrollment throughout the year at a glance.

Compare & Choose Plans

Medicare Plan Finder

medicare.gov

Compare Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medigap, and Part D plans side by side. Shows premiums, deductibles, coverage details, and out-of-pocket costs for your area.

Healthcare.gov Plan Comparison

healthcare.gov

Compare ACA Marketplace plans by premium, deductible, provider networks, and drug formularies. Shows estimated subsidy eligibility and out-of-pocket maximums.

MediPrimer: Choosing Coverage

mediprimer.org/choosing-coverage.html

A neutral framework for evaluating plans: how to weigh premiums vs. deductibles, in-network vs. out-of-network, and network size. Includes worksheets and common pitfalls.

MediPrimer: Plan Types

mediprimer.org/plan-types.html

What's the difference between Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, HMO, PPO, and PFFS? This overview explains how each plan type works and what you pay.

MediPrimer: Medigap (Supplemental Insurance)

mediprimer.org/medigap.html

How Medigap works, which plans cover what, and how to shop for the best rate. Includes enrollment windows and when you have guaranteed-issue rights.

Understand Costs & Get Help Paying

Medicare Cost Finder

medicare.gov

Look up specific doctors, hospitals, and providers to see what Medicare will pay and what you might owe. Helps estimate out-of-pocket costs before you enroll.

Benefits.gov Eligibility Screener

benefits.gov

Check your eligibility for Medicare Savings Programs (MSP), Extra Help with Part D costs, and other federal assistance programs based on income and resources.

Extra Help (Low-Income Subsidy for Part D)

1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) | ssa.gov

If you have limited income and resources, apply for Extra Help to reduce your Part D premiums and out-of-pocket drug costs. Administered by Social Security.

Medicare Savings Programs (State Medicaid)

mediprimer.org/state-medicaid.html

Qualify for help paying Medicare premiums and cost-sharing through your state Medicaid program. Income limits vary by state; apply through Medicaid, not Medicare.

MediPrimer: Understanding Your Costs

mediprimer.org/costs.html

Breakdown of premiums, deductibles, copays, coinsurance, and out-of-pocket maximums. Explains how each plan type sets costs and what drives them up or down.

MediPrimer: Getting Help Paying

mediprimer.org/getting-help.html

Overview of federal and state assistance programs: Extra Help, Medicare Savings Programs, Medicaid, CHIP, and charity programs. How to apply and what you'll need.

Check Eligibility

Benefits.gov Eligibility Screener

benefits.gov

Answer a short set of questions to find programs you may qualify for, including Medicare assistance, Medicaid, SNAP, housing, and utility programs.

Medicaid Eligibility by State

medicaid.gov

Medicaid eligibility varies widely by state. Look up your state's income limits, asset limits, and which populations are covered.

Healthcare.gov Eligibility Tool

healthcare.gov

Check if you qualify for ACA Marketplace coverage, estimate your subsidy, and see if you're eligible for Medicaid or CHIP in your state.

Medicare Eligibility at 65

1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) | medicare.gov

Most people become eligible for Medicare automatically at 65 if they have worked at least 40 quarters. Check your eligibility status and enrollment options online.

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)

1-800-772-1213 | ssa.gov

Workers with disabilities at any age can qualify for SSDI, which includes automatic Medicare eligibility after 24 months of benefits. Apply online or by phone.

MediPrimer: Coverage Basics

mediprimer.org/coverage-basics.html

Who qualifies for Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, and ACA Marketplace coverage. Covers age, disability status, citizenship, immigration status, and income tests.

MediPrimer: Medicaid Eligibility

mediprimer.org/medicaid-eligibility.html

Detailed overview of how Medicaid eligibility works, expansion states vs. non-expansion, and special programs like presumptive eligibility and continuous enrollment.

File a Complaint or Appeal

Medicare Appeals & Complaints

1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) | medicare.gov

File an appeal if Medicare or your plan denies a service or payment. Five-level appeal process for Original Medicare; separate process for Medicare Advantage and Part D.

State Insurance Departments

mediprimer.org/insurance-departments.html | naic.org

File a complaint about any health insurance company, Medicare Advantage plan, or Part D plan with your state regulator. Directory of all state departments and their contact info.

Report Fraud or Abuse

1-800-HHS-TIPS (1-800-447-8477) | oig.hhs.gov

Report suspected Medicare fraud, billing errors, or provider abuse to the HHS Office of Inspector General. Confidential hotline available 24/7.

State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP)

1-877-839-2675 | shiphelp.org

Free help navigating appeals, complaints, and coverage disputes. Local SHIP counselors can help you prepare your case and follow up with your plan.

MediPrimer: Your Rights

mediprimer.org/rights.html

Overview of your rights as a Medicare or Medicaid member, including the right to appeal denials, request explanations, and file complaints.

MediPrimer: Appeals & Timelines

mediprimer.org/appeals-timelines.html

Step-by-step explanations of the appeals process for each program: Medicare Original, Medicare Advantage, Part D, Medicaid. Includes deadlines and what happens at each stage.

MediPrimer: How Do I…?

mediprimer.org/how-do-i.html

Quick answers to common questions: how do I switch plans, appeal a denial, report fraud, change doctors, or request an exception to a coverage rule?
